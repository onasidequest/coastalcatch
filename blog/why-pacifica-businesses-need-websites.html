Why 68% of Pacifica Businesses Are Invisible Online

March 3, 2026 · By Coastal Catch Agency · 6 min read

Home / Pacifica Guide / Why Businesses Need Websites

We did something a little nerdy last month. We walked down Palmetto Ave and checked every single business for a website. Then we expanded to Manor Drive, Linda Mar, and the rest of Pacifica.

What we found was shocking -- but honestly, not surprising if you live here.

68%
of Pacifica small businesses have no website or an outdated one

That means roughly 7 out of 10 local businesses are essentially invisible to anyone searching online. And in 2026, that's most people.

What "Invisible" Actually Costs You

Let's make this real. Here's what happens when someone new moves to Pacifica (and about 800 people move to Pacifica or nearby areas every year):

  1. They need a haircut. They Google "barbershop near me."
  2. They need a plumber. They Google "plumber Pacifica."
  3. They want dinner. They Google "best restaurants Pacifica."

If your business doesn't show up? They go to your competitor who does. It's that simple.

97%
of consumers search online for local businesses (BrightLocal, 2025)

The Real Cost: Let's Do the Math

Say you're a barber charging $30 per haircut. If not having a website costs you just 3 new customers per week:

  • 3 customers x $30 = $90/week lost
  • $90 x 52 weeks = $4,680 per year in lost revenue
  • And that's conservative. Many of those customers would become regulars.

For a plumber or contractor? A single missed job could be worth $500 to $5,000. One website visitor who can't find you is potentially thousands in lost work.

"But I Have a Facebook Page"

We hear this a lot. And look, a Facebook page is better than nothing. But here's the truth:

  • Facebook reach is dying. Organic reach for business pages is under 5%. Your posts barely show up in anyone's feed anymore.
  • Google doesn't prioritize Facebook pages. When someone Googles "plumber Pacifica," your Facebook page isn't showing up on page one.
  • It looks unprofessional. When a customer Googles your business and finds a Facebook page instead of a real website, it signals "small" and "not established." Right or wrong, that's the perception.
  • You don't own it. Facebook can change their algorithm, restrict your page, or shut it down. Your website is yours.

"But I Get All My Business from Word of Mouth"

That's great, and word of mouth is powerful. But here's what happens after someone recommends you:

The person Googles your business name. If they find a professional website with your services, hours, and photos? They call you. If they find nothing? They second-guess the recommendation and start looking at alternatives.

A website doesn't replace word of mouth. It validates it.

✅ With a Website

  • ✅ Shows up in Google searches
  • ✅ Customers find you 24/7
  • ✅ New customers call or text
  • ✅ Reviews build trust
  • ✅ Looks established and credible
  • ✅ Generates leads on autopilot

It Doesn't Have to Be Complicated (or Expensive)

Here's the thing most web designers won't tell you: a small local business doesn't need a fancy 20-page website with animations and video backgrounds.

You need:

  • A clean homepage that says what you do
  • Your services listed clearly
  • Your phone number and address easy to find
  • Mobile-friendly design (70%+ of searches are on phones)
  • Google-optimized so you show up in local searches

That's it. A simple, professional website that works on every device and helps people find you.
