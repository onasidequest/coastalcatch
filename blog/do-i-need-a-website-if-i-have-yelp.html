Do I Need a Website If I Have a Yelp Page?

April 2, 2026 · By Coastal Catch Agency · 5 min read

Home / Pacifica Guide / Yelp vs. Website

Yes — you still need a website even with a Yelp page. Yelp shows your competitors' ads on your listing, you don't own the page, and Google ranks real websites above Yelp profiles for most local searches. A website gives you full control, better Google visibility, and a way to capture leads without paying Yelp.

We hear this question all the time from Pacifica business owners, usually accompanied by: "I have 47 Yelp reviews — isn't that enough?" It's a fair question. Yelp is free, you've built up reviews, and customers seem to find you there. Why spend money on a separate website?

Here's the honest answer.

What Yelp Is Good For (And What It's Not)

Yelp genuinely helps with social proof. When someone is already considering your business and wants to read reviews, Yelp delivers. That's valuable. But Yelp is a review directory, not a substitute for your own web presence.

❌ What Yelp Can't Do

  • You don't control your brand or design
  • Competitors' ads appear on your page
  • Limited ability to rank on Google
  • No way to capture emails or bookings
  • Yelp can suppress or hide reviews
  • Can't add your own content or blog posts
  • You don't own the page or data

✅ What Your Website Does

  • Full control of your brand and story
  • No competitors visible anywhere
  • Ranks for Google local searches
  • Online booking and contact forms
  • Your reviews, your way
  • Blog content that drives traffic
  • You own it forever

The Competitor Ad Problem

Here's something most business owners don't realize: Yelp places competitor ads directly on your free listing page.

When a customer visits your Yelp page, they see a section called "People Also Viewed" or "Sponsored" — showing your direct competitors. That means you're working hard to get Yelp reviews, and Yelp is using your popularity to send customers to your competition.

⚠️ Unless you pay Yelp ~$300–$500/month for advertising, your Yelp page is actively directing potential customers to your competitors. Your own website never does this.

Google Doesn't Rank Yelp Pages Like It Ranks Websites

When someone in Pacifica Googles "plumber near me" or "best hair salon Pacifica," here's the order Google shows results:

  1. Google Maps / Local Pack (your Google Business Profile)
  2. Individual business websites that are well-optimized
  3. Directory listings like Yelp, HomeAdvisor, etc.

Your Yelp page might appear on page 1, but it's below businesses that have real websites. And increasingly, AI-powered search tools like Google AI Overviews, ChatGPT, and Perplexity pull information from business websites — not Yelp listings.

If your only online presence is a Yelp page, you're invisible to a growing portion of how people search in 2026.

"But I Get Good Traffic From Yelp Already"

We're not saying abandon Yelp. Yelp traffic is real and valuable, especially for restaurants and service businesses. The point is that Yelp and a website work better together than either does alone.

Here's what a smart Pacifica business does:

  • Keep your Yelp page active and respond to reviews
  • Have your own website linked in your Yelp profile
  • Use your website to rank for Google searches Yelp can't capture
  • Capture email addresses through your website (Yelp doesn't let you do this)
  • Run promotions and showcase your full menu, gallery, or service list

What Yelp Cannot Give You (But a Website Can)

1. Online Booking

A website can integrate a real booking calendar so customers book appointments directly without calling. Yelp has a feature for this, but they control the flow and charge transaction fees.

2. Your Full Story

Yelp gives you a few hundred characters for a description. Your website gives you unlimited space to tell your story, show your team, display your work, and explain what makes your Pacifica business different.

3. Lead Capture

A contact form on your website sends inquiries directly to your email. You own that relationship. Yelp mediates it — and can stop showing your listing if you don't pay for ads.

4. Long-Term Asset

Your website is yours. You own the domain. Yelp can change their algorithm, restrict free listings, or shut down entirely. Your website keeps working regardless of what any platform decides to do.

Real Talk: What Happens in Pacifica

We've worked with business owners on Palmetto Ave and in Linda Mar who rely entirely on Yelp. They're getting some business — but they're also losing customers to competitors who show up first on Google with a professional website.

One local salon owner told us: "I thought my Yelp page was my website. I didn't realize people were Googling us and finding nothing." After we launched her site, she started getting calls from customers who found her on Google search — a channel that was completely dead for her before.

The Bottom Line: Use Both

Yelp and a website aren't competitors — they're teammates. Yelp builds review credibility. Your website builds Google visibility, brand trust, and lead capture. Together, they create a solid online presence that works for you 24/7.
