How to Get More Customers for My Pacifica Business

April 2, 2026 · By Coastal Catch Agency · 7 min read

Home / Pacifica Guide / Get More Customers

The highest-impact steps for getting more customers in Pacifica: get a mobile-friendly website, fully set up your Google Business Profile with photos and hours, actively collect Google reviews, and make it easy for people to contact or book you online. These four things alone can double your inbound inquiries within 90 days.

Pacifica is a close-knit community — but it's not immune to how people find businesses in 2026. Whether you're on Palmetto Ave, near Linda Mar Beach, or tucked away in a neighborhood, your customers are searching online before they drive anywhere.

Here's a practical, priority-ordered guide for getting more customers without a big marketing budget.

Step 1: Get Found on Google (The Highest ROI Move)

1

Claim and Complete Your Google Business Profile

It's free. Go to business.google.com and claim your listing. Add your hours, phone number, address, services, and at least 10 photos. Businesses with complete profiles get 7x more clicks than incomplete ones.

2

Build a Website That Ranks Locally

A simple, well-built website tells Google exactly what you do and where you do it. Make sure your site mentions "Pacifica, CA" and your services clearly on the homepage. This alone can push you into local search results within weeks.

3

Get More Google Reviews (This Week)

Text 5 recent happy customers and ask for a Google review. Just say: "Hey [name], if you have a moment, I'd really appreciate a Google review — it helps a lot: [your Google review link]." Aim for 10+ reviews to see a noticeable boost in rankings.

In short: before a new customer walks through your door, they've already looked you up. If they find nothing, or find a competitor with more reviews and a better website, they call them instead.

Step 2: Make It Easy to Contact You

You'd be amazed how many businesses lose customers simply because it's hard to find their phone number. Make sure these are easy to find everywhere:

  • Phone number in the top section of your website (not buried in a contact page)
  • Contact form on your site for people who prefer not to call
  • Text messaging option — many customers in Pacifica prefer to text (we can set this up for you)
  • Your hours, especially if they're seasonal or vary
  • Your address with a Google Maps link

Every friction point between "I want to contact this business" and "I just sent them a message" is a potential lost customer. Remove every obstacle you can.

Step 3: Build a Review Engine

Reviews are the new word of mouth. When someone in Pacifica asks "Who's a good plumber around here?" on Nextdoor, the first answer is often "They have great Yelp/Google reviews." Your review count matters.

Here's a simple system that works:

  1. After every completed job or service, ask: "Was everything great today?"
  2. If yes: "Would you mind leaving us a quick Google review? It really helps us a lot."
  3. Send them a direct link to your Google review form (you can find this in your Google Business dashboard)
  4. Aim for 2–3 new reviews per month

Businesses with 20+ Google reviews rank significantly higher in local search results than businesses with just a few. It snowballs — more reviews → higher ranking → more customers → more reviews.

Step 4: Use Nextdoor and Local Facebook Groups

Pacifica has an active Nextdoor community and local Facebook groups. These are goldmines for local businesses:

  • Claim your business on Nextdoor (it's free)
  • Respond when people ask for recommendations in your category
  • Post occasional updates — a seasonal special, a new service, a photo of recent work
  • Join the "Pacifica, CA" Facebook groups and participate genuinely

The key word here is genuinely. Nobody likes spam. But if someone asks "Does anyone know a good electrician in Pacifica?" and you respond helpfully, you're not spamming — you're being a community resource.

Step 5: Create a Simple Referral Program

Word of mouth is still the most powerful marketing in a tight-knit community like Pacifica. You can accelerate it with a simple referral incentive:

  • "Refer a friend and get $20 off your next service"
  • "Bring a friend to your next appointment and both of you get 10% off"
  • For contractors: "Refer someone who books a project and earn a $50 gift card"

Print cards with the offer, add it to receipts, text it to past customers. It doesn't take a big budget — just intentionality.

Step 6: Show Up Consistently on One Social Platform

You don't need to be on every platform. Pick one that makes sense for your business and post 2–3 times per week:

  • Instagram — great for restaurants, salons, fitness, anything visual
  • Facebook — works well for service businesses (plumbers, cleaners, contractors) with an older audience
  • Nextdoor — best for hyper-local service businesses

What to post: before/after photos of your work, behind-the-scenes of your day, customer spotlights (with permission), seasonal promotions, and local content relevant to Pacifica.

What Not to Waste Time On (At Least Not Yet)

Small business owners have limited time. Here's what to deprioritize until you've nailed the basics:

  • Paid advertising (expensive and hard to get right without good fundamentals)
  • TikTok (unless you're in a visual/entertainment niche)
  • Email newsletters (valuable, but builds slowly)
  • SEO blog content (great long-term strategy, low short-term return)

Get the fundamentals right first: website, Google Business Profile, reviews. Then layer in additional strategies.

The Pacifica Advantage

Here's something unique about Pacifica: it's a community that genuinely supports local businesses. Linda Mar surfers stop at local coffee shops. Palmetto Ave shoppers prefer local over chains. Families on Fassler Ave use neighborhood plumbers and handymen.

If you show up professionally online, you're already ahead of the 68% of Pacifica businesses that don't have a proper web presence. The bar is lower than you think. A clean website, good reviews, and a complete Google profile puts you in the top third of local businesses for online visibility.
